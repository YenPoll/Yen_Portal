## Project Overview

**Organization:** Wacker Neuson Machinery  
**Role:** Product Marketing Manager  
**Timeline:** June 2019 — January 2020  
**Team Size:** 20+ cross-functional members  
**Budget:** Up to $1M  
**Methodology:** Agile / Scrum

Wacker Neuson's product data was fragmented across multiple legacy systems, creating inconsistencies across e-commerce, marketing, sales, and supply chain channels. With 500+ active SKUs and no single source of truth, the cost of inaccurate or incomplete product information was compounding across every customer touchpoint.

This project consolidated that data into a unified Product Information Management (PIM) platform — with structured data taxonomy, standardized attributes, and channel-specific publishing rules designed for global and LATAM market operations.

## Challenge

The organization faced several interconnected data and operations problems:

- **Fragmented product data** spread across multiple legacy systems with no central governance
- **500+ SKUs** with inconsistent attributes, naming conventions, and channel-specific requirements
- **Six departments** — Marketing, E-commerce, IT, Supply Chain, Sales, and Product — operating from different data sources
- **No structured taxonomy** for product classification or attribute standardization
- **E-commerce underperformance** driven by incomplete and inaccurate product listings

## Approach

The project followed Agile/Scrum methodology with structured phases covering discovery, requirement prioritization, platform configuration, and launch validation.

### Discovery & Stakeholder Alignment

Conducted in-depth stakeholder interviews across six departments to map current-state pain points, data ownership gaps, and channel-specific requirements. Documented workflows, exception handling, and manual workarounds that had accumulated around the legacy systems.

### Requirement Gathering & Prioritization

Facilitated process-mapping workshops to translate business needs into prioritized user stories. Backlog items were ranked against business impact, data completeness requirements, and channel readiness — ensuring the platform was built for real operational use from day one.

### Cross-Functional Partnership

Partnered with IT, Marketing, E-commerce, and Supply Chain teams to define:

- Data taxonomy and product classification hierarchy
- Attribute standards and validation rules
- Channel-specific publishing requirements (web, print, distributor portals)
- Integration architecture with existing ERP and e-commerce platforms

### Business Case & Executive Alignment

Refined the business case in alignment with executive stakeholders, mapping projected ROI to specific operational improvements. Secured full project approval and budget sign-off by connecting data quality outcomes to measurable commercial impact.

## Results

- **20% improvement** in e-commerce conversion metrics driven by enhanced product data completeness and accuracy
- **500+ SKUs** successfully consolidated from fragmented legacy systems into a single, governed platform
- **Six departments** aligned on a shared data taxonomy and attribute standard for the first time
- **Reduced manual data maintenance** across Marketing, E-commerce, and Supply Chain teams
- **Full executive approval and budget sign-off** secured through a structured, ROI-aligned business case

## Key Takeaway

Product data is a commercial asset — not a back-office function. When 500+ SKUs carry inconsistent, incomplete, or ungoverned information across channels, the cost compounds silently through conversion loss, customer frustration, and operational rework. Structured PIM implementation is a revenue and margin intervention, not just a technology project.

Securing cross-functional alignment before platform configuration — through structured workshops, stakeholder interviews, and prioritized user stories — was the foundation that made the post-launch results achievable.
